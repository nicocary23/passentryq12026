🚀 Self-Serve & Automated Billing

In November 2025 we flipped the switch on self-serve sign-ups. Any business in the world can now discover PassEntry, create an account and start issuing passes without ever speaking to a human. 32 clients signed through self-serve to date. Acquisition cost near zero. Sales cycle near zero.

In October 2025 we launched automated billing. Within 21 days, 14 clients had a card on file and were in trial. We have since introduced custom pricing plans — enabling fully tailored commercial arrangements for any client, from a small self-serve account to a complex enterprise deal with volume tiers. The roadmap: smarter dunning, automated renewals, usage-based billing, and annual contract incentives. We are building the billing infrastructure that scales with us as we go from 87 clients to 500+.

🏢 Access Control & Campus

Access control has always meant compromise — physical keys, plastic fobs, proprietary systems that lock you in. We're changing that. PassEntry delivers complete access infrastructure, from hardware and installation through to digital credentials in Apple and Google Wallet. Clients choose what works for them. We make it work.

QR code access gives instant, flexible entry for hotels, aparthotels, guest passes and short-term stays. No app downloads. No specialist hardware. Passes issued in seconds, revoked just as fast. Our first case study: StayCity — Europe's fastest growing aparthotel group — using PassEntry for digital check-in across their Dublin locations.

NFC tap-to-unlock is the long-term play. Tap your phone, door opens. No scanning, no screen interaction, bank-grade security. Ideal for offices, residential buildings, student accommodation and care homes. Ashbury & Bloom, a high-end property developer in South-East England, is already deploying our NFC access across their residential portfolio.

This is not a feature. It is an entirely new revenue category — one where PassEntry owns the full stack from hardware to wallet credential. Every building that runs on our access infrastructure is a client that never leaves.

🕶️ Special Ops: Secure Element

We are issuing proprietary payment credentials directly into the iPhone and Android using Secure Element — the same hardware chip that powers Apple Pay and Google Pay. Apple opened third-party access in 2024. We moved into that gap. Face ID, tap, instant payment, zero interchange. First potential client: Frasers Group.

This is not a feature. This is a category shift — from pass infrastructure to the payment layer itself.
